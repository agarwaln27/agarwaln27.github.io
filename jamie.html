Jamie: Emotional Regulation & Life-Coach App
CMU Course, Persuasive Design

Timeline

3 months, October to December 2018

Target Users

Young Working Professionals

My Role
  • User research
  • Literature review
  • Concept generation & testing
  • Visual design

Overview

Using physiological data and text-based input to trigger timely and contextual interventions to help user manage some of life's stressful moments.

Challenge

How can we help young working professionals use their physiological and contextual data to better understand their emotional triggers and learn strategies to manage stressful reactions that may result from them?

Solution

Know Yourself Demo video

Process

For the final project of our Persuasive Design class, my teammates and I arrived at the idea of Jamie by conducting extensive secondary research. We were interested in emotional regulation from the start and learned many interesting things about bio-sensing and strategies for dealing with negative emotions.

We learned about the concepts of approach and avoidance. Everyone has a style they follow when managing challenges. While both have their benefits, avoidance techniques alone will not resolve the challenges entirely. We took this into account in our design in the way we paired our activities. Through physiological and bio-sensing research, we learned different methods to measure emotional state. This includes user input, contextual data, skin conductance, and heart rate. Using both types of data, users may develop effective emotional regulation strategies.
